Lesson 4 covered left-borders, now in Lesson 5 we're to create a double border. There are (at least) two ways to create a page that has a double border, and this is the second.

The second way to do this is to use a single background tile and center the content in the middle of it. This gives an entire different look to the page. You can see what I mean on this page.

I used two background graphic for this page, the patterned background and a small tile with the solid color for the inside of the table. Instead of using the clear graphic to space things out, I simply centered a small table on the page.

The content is in a table that is 80% of the width of the cell it is in, with a center alignment so that the text moves away from the borders. Of course, I can't count on the borders being covered in such a way that I get columns as I can with the other method.

The design that I used for the background of this page is yet another piece out of one of my clip art collections, trimmed in such a way that it is seamless. Don't you find it ridiculous how many clip art collections give you background tiles that don't tile properly?

Now you can return to the "double border" method.
