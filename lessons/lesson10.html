Lesson 10 is all about using Java Applets. In addition to this page, I have created two other pages. One uses a Lake Applet (I'm sure you've seen one of those, it's the kind that makes it look as though there's moving water in the picture.) and the other uses a Snow Applet (to make it look as though it is snowing in the picture.)

Take a look...

  • Lake Applet
  • Snow Applet

The advantage to applets is that you can use them to do things with your web page that would be difficult to do otherwise because Java works with most browsers and most operating systems. Of course, by using a Java applet, you greatly increase the amount of time it takes to load the page containing the applet.

Now, just for fun, I've added an applet that I found at JavaFile.com (a great place to find Java Applets, JavaScripts, and DHTML to add to your pages, by the way.) It's called "Goldfish" and you can have a little fun with it if you move your mouse over the picture...
