Overview

Creating a website involves more than sitting down and starting to type stuff. Take a look at the steps I follow when creating a new website, whether for myself or someone else.

Define the Project

Think about the following when roughing out the design. You need to define:

  • The audience - Who are the visitors to this website? You need to understand who will be viewing the site and what they are expecting the site to do for them.Is the average visitor an experienced computer user or a beginner? Perhaps the site will provide services for children?
  • The purpose the site will serve for its customers. Do you need to make a sale or are visitors to the site ready to buy something you are selling. If the site will be providing information only, what information are they looking for? Keep it on topic! Are they looking for information or some item they want to buy from you.
  • Do you expect to sign up customers for a newsletter or just to simplify their future visits to the website? What information will you need from visitors and how are you going to gain their trust enough to make them want to give you that information?

Once you know what visitors need, and what you need to provide to satisfy their needs, you can go on the next step in website design...

Plan the Content

Once you have defined the goal and identified your audience, you can plan out the site's content:

  • Define the site structure

    It is easier to visualize the structure of a website if you create a diagram using an application like Visio.

  • Define the navigation
  • Select a color scheme
  • Gather the images, videos, and icons
  • Write the text for the site

    Now is the time to write the text your visitors will see on the site.

Sketch the Design

Now that you have defined the content, you can sketch out the design.

Lay out the content so it is organized according to the site structure, i.e. information about the site on the About page, information about products on the product page, etc.
