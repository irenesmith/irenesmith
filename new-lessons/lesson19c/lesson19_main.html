Lesson 19, the next to the last, is all about frames. This page uses frames. The menu on the left is in a separate frame from the content section here on the right. Clearly the advantage is that the menu on the left will stay in view at all times, even if the content of this frame is longer than the height of the screen. You can scroll this side of the window up and down without disturbing the menu at all.

Unless your monitor is really small and you have to view this page at 640x480 pixels, there won't be a scrollbar on the menu frame, but the content frame will have a scrollbar when it needs one.

Probably the biggest disadvantage of a framed site, of course, is the fact that you can't bookmark individual pages, only the page that contains the frameset. Sometimes, however, this disadvantage is an advantage because it forces visitors to start in the same place on every visit.

The homework for this lesson was to load more than one page into this frame, to show how it works and that's what I've done. If you look at the menu, you'll see that you can go back to Lesson 18, go to the index of my Portfolio, or go ahead to Lesson 19. These links will load in the full screen, in other words, without the frames.
