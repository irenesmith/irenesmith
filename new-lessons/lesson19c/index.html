Lesson 19, the next to the last, is all about frames. I have left an unedited copy of the two pages I originally made that use frames so you can see them. However, you probably shouldn't use frames.

Why shouldn't I use frames?

Besides the fact that frames are no longer a part of the HTMl 5 specification, frames have other disadvantages as well:

  • If you want to make your website accessible, using frames will get in the way. Frames are not conducive to an accessible site.
  • Search engines won't properly scan your site and the page that is linked from the search engine listing may not be the one you want your visitors to see. If the listing points directly to one of your content pages. The page may load without a menu or some other vital content missing.
  • HTML 5 does not support frames. You can use iFrames in HTML 5, but traditional frames and framesets are not allowed.
  • Probably the biggest disadvantage of a framed site, of course, is the fact that you can't bookmark individual pages, only the page that contains the frameset. Sometimes, however, this disadvantage is an advantage because it forces visitors to start in the same place on every visit.
